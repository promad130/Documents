Product of Sums (POS) in Karnaugh Maps

Understanding Product of Sums (POS)

What is POS Form?

Product of Sums (POS) form expresses Boolean functions as a product of sum terms, where each sum corresponds to a maxterm (input combination that makes the function 0).

Key Concepts

  • Maxterms: Sum terms that make the function equal to 0
  • Grouping: We group 0s in the K-map (opposite of SOP)
  • Result: Product of sum expressions like (A+B)(A'+C)

Maxterm Explanation

Maxterm Mx corresponds to input combination x that makes function = 0. For example:

M0 = A+B+C+D (makes F=0 when A=0,B=0,C=0,D=0)
M2 = A+B+C'+D (makes F=0 when A=0,B=0,C=1,D=0)
M5 = A+B'+C+D' (makes F=0 when A=0,B=1,C=0,D=1)

Step-by-Step POS Example

Example: F(A,B,C,D) = π(0,2,5,7)

Step 1: Understand the Notation

π(0,2,5,7) means Product of maxterms M0, M2, M5, and M7

M0 = (A+B+C+D)
M2 = (A+B+C'+D)
M5 = (A+B'+C+D')
M7 = (A+B'+C'+D')

Step 2: Place 0s in K-Map

Place 0s at positions 0, 2, 5, 7. All other positions get 1s.

Step 3: Group Adjacent 0s

Group 1: {0, 2} - Adjacent horizontally

Group 2: {5, 7} - Adjacent horizontally

Step 4: Derive Sum Terms

For Group 1 {0, 2}: Variables that don't change → (A+B+D)

For Group 2 {5, 7}: Variables that don't change → (A+B'+D')

Final POS Expression

F = (A+B+D)(A+B'+D')
