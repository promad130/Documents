## 1. Material Implication
- **Format:** $(A→B)$
- **Meaning:** “If A, then B.”
- **Truth Table:** False only when A is true and B is false.
- **Usage:** Standard in propositional logic.

## 2. Converse
- **Format:** $(B→A)$
- **Meaning:** Switches the roles of A and B.
- **Note:** The truth of the implication does not guarantee the converse is true.

## 3. Inverse
- **Format:** $((¬A)→(¬B))$
- **Meaning:** “If not A, then not B.”
- **Note:** Not logically equivalent to the original implication.

## 4. Contrapositive
- **Format:** ($(¬B)→(¬A)$)
- **Meaning:** “If not B, then not A.”
- **Note:** Logically equivalent to the original implication.

## 5. Bi-implication (Biconditional)
- **Format:** (A↔B)
- **Meaning:** “A if and only if B.”
- **Truth Table:** True when A and B have the same truth value.

---
## Additional Context
- **Strict Implication:** Used in modal logic, based on necessity, not just truth values.
- **Logical Equivalence:** Not an implication, but denotes mutual implication $(A↔B)$.
