Now, another piller of [[Class or Type]] is Abstraction.

For example you are watching a video on youtube, for you it is simple to watch that video, all you need to do is select the video you want to watch and wuala, the video is playing, and all you need to worry about is pause/play, rewind, and playback speed and all. 
But each of that function is being executed by a really complex system behind the screen. Of course the developers have to keep that complex system away from you otherwise you will be scared, that is why they created the buttons to do things you want to do hiding everything that is going in the back as soon as you play that button using a layer called **"ABSTRACTION"**.

So whenever i chose a video to play, I don't want to select which frame to play from, what should be the frame rate, what should be the downloading speed and upload speed, what should be the ideal resolution and how loud should be the video, and in order to  hide those details from me, a layer of abstraction is added so that the video that I want to play makes perfect sense to me, i.e., I click and the video play.

Abstraction exists everywhere in the world, everything has a layer of abstraction in order to make it simpler for others who aren't known to it to use.

So, all we try to do is make the operation system simple for others to use.

First, for example we want to take a selfie, now the code for the selfie itself is way to complex, so what we do is make another `abstract class` which will allow user to actually access the complex code without ever actually interacting with the complex part of the process.
