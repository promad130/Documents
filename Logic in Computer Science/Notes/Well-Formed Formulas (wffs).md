## What are WFFs (Well-Formed Formulas) in Logic?
A **wff** (well-formed formula) is a string of symbols constructed according to the rules of a **logical system’s syntax**. It’s a formula that is _constructed correctly_ with respect to the allowed operations and structure.

## Key Features of WFFs
- **Grammatically correct:** A wff obeys all the formation rules of the logic system.
- **Made from atomic propositions and logical connectives:** You use symbols like p,q, and operators such as AND (∧∧), OR (∨∨), NOT (¬¬), IMPLIES (→→), etc.
- **Can be simple or complex:** A single proposition (like pp) is a wff. So are more complex combinations (like (p∧q)→r(p∧q)→r).

## Examples
- **Atomic wff:** $p$
- **Compound wff:** $p\land q$
- **Nested wff:** $(p→q)∧(¬r)$
- **NOT a wff:** $pq∧$ (no clear structure or missing parentheses)

## Why Are WFFs Important?
WFFs make sure you’re working with formulas that can be **reasoned about** logically.
- Only wffs can be assigned a truth value.
- They are the _valid inputs_ for logic operations, truth tables, and proofs.
