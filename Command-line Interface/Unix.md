Unix is a family of multitasking, multi-user computer operating systems that originated from the original AT&T Unix, which was first developed in 1969 at Bell Labs by Ken Thompson, Dennis Ritchie, and others.

It was initially designed as a convenient platform for programmers and quickly grew in popularity, especially in academic and research settings, due to its simplicity, portability, and powerful features
